APPLICATION TERMS AND CRITERIA
To apply for halal certifcation, food premise must possess a valid Business Registration Certificate from SSM or Cooperative Registration Certificate under SKM or Establishment Document under the Act of Parliament or the respective Ministry and other Government agencies
To apply for halal certifcation, food premise must possess a license or approval from the local authority or a valid confirmation / letter of support from any Government agency
To apply for halal certifcation, food premise must possess a valid Food Premises Registration Certificate from BKKM
To apply for halal certifcation, food premise must have a fully operational for at least 3 months prior to applying for SPHM for new applications
To apply for halal certifcation, food premise must have a fully operational for at least 1 month in the new premises for the relocating company
To apply for halal certifcation, food premise must be issuing or operating halal-only menus and complying with the set halal standards
To apply for halal certifcation, food premise must apply to SPHM for all types of menus that are still being produced at the premises
To apply for halal certifcation, food premise must submit supporting documents clearly stating the source of the material (for natural materials only)
To apply for halal certifcation, the name of the company, menu, and ingredients requested must not use the statement 'halal' or any other representation or description to indicate that it can be used by Muslims

Non-halal products are not not eligible to apply
Companies that produce or distribute halal and non-halal menus are not not eligible to apply
Does not have standards or guidelines to use as a reference are not not eligible to apply
Food premises that have negative religious and social implications such as karaoke centers and entertainment centers are not not eligible to apply
Use of company names and menus that refer to non-halal or synonymous with or resemble non-halal products or any misleading terms such as ham, bak kut teh, bacon, beer, rum, hot dog, charsiew and the like are not not eligible to apply
Menus that can lead to deviations of faith, superstitions and fraud are not not eligible to apply
The hotel has a kitchen prepare or based pork serve menu are not not eligible to apply

For fees payment, each application under the Malaysian Halal Certification (Domestic) is charged a fee of RM20.00 only
For fees payment, each application involving foreign companies/applicants conducting product manufacturing within the country is subject to a fee of RM200.00 only.
For fees payment, industry that has an annual income less than RM300,000.00 is considered as micro industry has to pay a fee of RM 200 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, industry that has an annual income from RM300,000.00 to RM 15,000,000.00 is considered as small industry has to pay a fee of RM 800 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, industry that has an annual income from RM 15,000,000.00 to RM 50,000,000.00 is considered as medium industry has to pay a fee of RM 1400 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, industry that has an annual income more than RM50,000,000.00 is considered as large industry has to pay a fee of RM 2000 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, for foreign industry that is the food and drinks, cosmetic and pharmaseutical, used goods and medical devices business in ASEAN has to pay a fee of RM2500 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, for foreign industry that is the food and drinks, cosmetic and pharmaseutical, used goods and medical devices business not in ASEAN has to pay a fee of RM10000 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, MUIS has the authority to exempt fee payments for any applications that are fully managed by any government agency.

Each new application and renewal of food premises such as restaurants or cafes, canteens, bakery, chained food premises, mobile premises, cafeterias in food courts, kiosks are subject to the RM 200 for each premises
For each kitchen or restaurant in a 4 or higher stars hotel need to pay a fee of 1000
For each kitchen or restaurant in a 3 or lower stars hotel need to pay a fee of 200

For food premise scheme on catering / food serving services / convocation centre kitchen on a small scale which has a yearly income less than RM 500,000.00 has to pay a fee of RM 200 for applying new food premises certification
For food premise scheme on catering / food serving services / convocation centre kitchen on a medium scale which has a yearly income range from RM 500,000.00 to RM  5,000,000.00 has to pay a fee of RM 800 for applying new food premises certification
For food premise scheme on catering / food serving services / convocation centre kitchen on a large scale which has a yearly income above RM  5,000,000.00 has to pay a fee of RM 1400 for applying new food premises certification

Each application for adding menus including promotional menus will be charged a fee of RM 200.00 only for a period of 2 years
MUIS reserves the right to waive the payment of fees for any application that is fully managed by any government agency

For halal certification payment method, companies/applicants must make the payment within 14 working days after receiving the charge letter through the MYeHALAL system.

Each premises must have a valid Food Premises Registration Certificate from BKKM

Chain food premises that use the same brand must apply for SPHM for each chain of premises and the application must be managed centrally
The menu declared on the MYeHALAL application form must be the same as stated on the menu display
Must apply for SPHM for all menus prepared at food premises. Menus that are not declared must be applied for menu addition
MHMS should be developed and implemented effectively with reference to the MHMS Manual 2020

RENEWAL
Applications for SPHM must be made online through the MYeHALAL system at www.halal.gov.my/v4.
Applications for SPHM renewal must be submitted as early as 6 months and no later than 3 months before the SPHM expires.
Applications for SPHM renewal that have expired should be categorized as new applications.
The company/applicant should refer to the requirements for filling out the MYeHALAL application form and any addition of food premises and new menus is not allowed.
The company/applicant should refer to the requirements for filling out the MYeHALAL application form and any update of information related to product names and brands must be officially notified to MUIS.
A summary of raw materials or ingredients must be submitted to facilitate the identification of changes in renewal applications and previous applications.
Incomplete applications and those that do not comply with the correct Malaysian Halal Certification scheme and category will be rejected by MUIS.
Applications will be managed by MUIS based on the Malaysian Halal Certification scheme and the manufacturer's address. The company/applicant can find out the officer handling the application based on the information displayed in the MYeHALAL system user account.
The company/applicant is not allowed to apply for a change of authority responsible for handling the SPHM application.
Complete supporting documents must be submitted to MUIS (as stated in the applicant's MYeHALAL system) within 5 working days after the MYeHALAL application form is submitted online.
Fees must be paid within 14 working days after receiving the fee payment letter.
A field audit will be conducted after the fee is received by MUIS.

ADDITION
Applications for menu additions and promotional menus are open to companies/applicants.
Companies/applicants must submit an application for menu additions or promotional menus only for food premises that are still valid and relevant.
SPHM applications must be made online through the MYeHALAL system at www.halal.gov.my/v4.
The MYeHALAL application form must be filled out completely and all information must be filled in accurately, thoroughly, comprehensively, and correctly. This includes company information, raw materials, suppliers, products, menus, and other related details.
The MYeHALAL application form must be filled out completely and raw materials or ingredients and products that use codes must include the actual name (not just the trade name).
The MYeHALAL application form must be filled out completely and the name of the menu being applied for must be filled in exactly as it appears on the menu display list.
The MYeHALAL application form must be filled out completely and menu names in foreign languages must be translated into Malay and/or English.
Incomplete applications and those that do not follow the correct Malaysian Halal Certification scheme will be rejected by MUIS.
Applications will be managed based on the Malaysian Halal Certification scheme and the manufacturer's address. The company/applicant can find out the officer handling the application based on the information displayed in the MYeHALAL system account.
The company/applicant is not allowed to apply for a change of authority responsible for handling SPHM.
Complete supporting documents must be submitted to MUIS (as stated in the applicant's MYeHALAL system) within 5 working days after the MYeHALAL application form is submitted online.
Fees must be paid within 14 working days after receiving the fee payment letter.
A field audit will be conducted after the fee is received by MUIS.
The use of approved additional menus or promotional menus must be subject to the SPHM of food premises that are still valid.

MERGING
Any application for the merging of SPHM must be classified as a new application.
The merging of valid or new SPHM (if applicable) is allowed for food and beverage product schemes.
Applications for SPHM merging must be submitted within 6 months before the certification expiry date.
The merging of SPHM must involve all products on the valid SPHM.
Applications for SPHM merging must be accompanied by a cover letter clearly stating the details of the previous application.
The procedure for applying for SPHM merging must follow the procedure for New Registration.
The valid SPHM will be canceled if the merging application is approved by MUIS.
Canceled SPHM must be returned to MUIS.
