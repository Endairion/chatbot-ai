To apply for halal certifcation, one must possess a valid Business Registration Certificate from SSM or a Cooperative Registration Certificate under SKM, or a Founding Document under the relevant Act of Parliament or Ministry, and other Government agencies.
To apply for halal certifcation, one must have a valid license or approval from PBT or a valid confirmation/support letter from any Government agency.
To apply for halal certifcation, one must possess a valid Food Premises Registration Certificate from BKKM.
To apply for halal certifcation, one must be fully operational and marketed for at least 3 months before applying for SPHM for new applications.
To apply for halal certifcation, one must be fully operational for at least 1 month at the new premises for companies that have relocated.
To apply for halal certifcation, one must produce or handle only halal products, including trading products, and comply with the specified halal standards.
To apply for halal certifcation, one must apply for SPHM for all types of products still being produced at the said premises.
To apply for halal certifcation, one must submit a recognized and valid halal certificate for repackaged products or provide clear supporting documents stating the source of materials (for natural materials only).
To apply for halal certifcation, The company name, product name, ingredients, and brand applied for must not use the statement 'halal' or any representation or be described in any other way to indicate it can be used by Muslims.

Non-halal products are not not eligible to apply
Companies that produce or distribute both halal and non-halal products are not not eligible to apply
Use of the same brand for halal and non-halal products or services (subject to application in Malaysia) are not not eligible to apply
Products that do not have standards or guidelines for reference are not not eligible to apply
Products that have a negative impact on religion and social aspects such as drugs, shisha, cigarettes, hair dyes, and nail polish are not not eligible to apply
Unprocessed natural products, unmodified, and without packaging and labeling used such as fresh fish, fresh vegetables, and fresh eggs are not not eligible to apply
Use of company names, products, and brands that refer to non-halal products or are synonymous with or resemble non-halal products or any misleading terms such as ham, bak kut teh, bacon, beer, rum, hot dog, char siew, and similar items are not not eligible to apply
Finished products from domestic and foreign countries that are relabeled without undergoing any process in Malaysia are not not eligible to apply
Products in the early stages of R&D that are not yet ready for commercialization are not not eligible to apply
Products that can lead to deviation of faith, superstitious beliefs, and fraud are not not eligible to apply
Products that do not need to be halal certified or can cause confusion if halal certified such as fertilizers, paper, cement, tiles, insecticides, and carpets are not not eligible to apply

For fees payment, each application under the Malaysia Halal Certification (Domestic) is subject to a fee of RM20.00 only.
For fees payment, each application involving foreign companies/applicants conducting product manufacturing within the country is subject to a fee of RM200.00 only.
For fees payment, industry that has an annual income less than RM300,000.00 is considered as micro industry has to pay a fee of RM 200 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, industry that has an annual income from RM300,000.00 to RM 15,000,000.00 is considered as small industry has to pay a fee of RM 800 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, industry that has an annual income from RM 15,000,000.00 to RM 50,000,000.00 is considered as medium industry has to pay a fee of RM 1400 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, industry that has an annual income more than RM50,000,000.00 is considered as large industry has to pay a fee of RM 2000 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, for foreign industry that is the food and drinks, cosmetic and pharmaseutical, used goods and medical devices business in ASEAN has to pay a fee of RM2500 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, for foreign industry that is the food and drinks, cosmetic and pharmaseutical, used goods and medical devices business not in ASEAN has to pay a fee of RM10000 for new application and renewal
For fees payment, MUIS has the authority to exempt fee payments for any applications that are fully managed by any government agency.

For halal certification payment method, companies/applicants must make the payment within 14 working days after receiving the charge letter through the MYeHALAL system.
For halal certification payment method, the fee payment must be made via online payment, QR code, or cheque only.
For halal certification payment method, payments should be made payable to Tetuan Majlis Ugama Islam Sabah.
For halal certification payment method, a receipt for each payment made must be issued and submitted to MUIS.
For halal certification payment method, Fees paid are non-refundable and non-transferable to other applications.
For halal certification payment method, MUIS reserves the right to impose additional fees if the fee determination information provided by the company/applicant is inaccurate.

Each premise must have a valid Food Premises Registration Certificate from BKKM.
Each food handler must have a Food Handling Training Certificate from an institution recognized by KKM.
Each food handler must obtain a valid anti-typhoid vaccination from a registered medical practitioner.
Each product containing a combination of food and drugs (food-drug interphase) must submit a product classification letter from NPRA (if applicable).
The requirements of MS 1500:2019 Halal Food-General Requirements (Third Revision), Food Act 1983 (Act 281), Food Regulations 1985, Food Hygiene Regulations 2009 and other latest laws and regulations enforced by the relevant authorities must be complied with
Companies/applicants who are eligible to apply for the food and beverage product scheme are entrepreneur or manufacturer, repackaging entrepreneur, centralized kitchen entrepreneur and brand owner

In the micro industry category, a minimum of 1 Malaysian Muslim employee, holding a permanent position must be appointed and work in the processing area during the operation period or shift
In the micro industry category, IHCS must be developed and implemented based on the MHMS Manual 2020

In the small industry category, A minimum of 1 Malaysian Muslim employee, holding a permanent position must be appointed and work in the processing area during the operation period or shift
In the small industry category, A permanent Halal Supervisor must be appointed at the premises
In the small industry category, IHCS must be developed and implemented based on the MHMS Manual 2020

In the medium and large industry category, A minimum of 2 Malaysian Muslim employees, holding permanent positions must be appointed and work in the processing area during the operation period or shift
In the medium and large industry category, Permanent Halal Executives must be appointed at each branch of the premises
In the medium and large industry category, JKHD shall be established for premises and each branch of the premises has different operational management
In the medium and large industry category, HAS must be built and implemented based on the MHMS Manual 2020

RENEWAL
SPHM application must be done online through the MYeHALAL system at www.halal.gov.my/v4
Applications for renewal of SPHM must be submitted as early as 6 months and at least 3 months before the expiry of the SPHM period
An expired SPHM renewal application must be categorised as a new application
Companies/applicants should refer to the requirements for filling in the MYeHALAL application form and any addition of products is not allowed
Companies/applicants should refer to the requirements for filling in the MYeHALAL application form and any update of information related to product names and brands must submit an official notification to MUIS
A summary of raw materials or ingredients should be submitted to facilitate the identification of changes in renewal applications and previous applications
Applications that are incomplete and do not follow the correct Malaysian Halal Certification scheme and category will be rejected by MUIS
Applications will be managed by MUIS based on the Malaysian Halal Certification scheme and the manufacturer's address. The company/applicant can find out the officer who manages the application based on the information stated in the user account of the MYeHALAL system
The company/applicant is not allowed to apply for the change of the competent authority that manages the SPHM application
Completed supporting documents must be submitted to MUIS (as stated in the applicant's MYeHALAL system) within 5 working days after the MYeHALAL application form is submitted online.
Payment of the fee must be cleared within 14 working days upon receipt of the fee payment letter
The field audit will be carried out after the fee payment is received by MUIS

ADDITION
Addition of products in applications is not open for food and beverage product schemes

MERGER
Any application for incorporation of SPHM shall be classified as a new application
Applications for SPHM merger that are still valid or new (if relevant) are allowed for food and beverage product schemes
Applications for SPHM incorporation must be submitted within 6 months prior to the expiry of the certification date
The merger of SPHM must involve all products in the SPHM that are still valid
The application for SPHM merger must be accompanied by a cover letter clearly stating the details of the previous application
The procedure for applying for the incorporation of SPHM shall follow the procedure of the New Registration
The SPHM that is still valid must be void if the application for merger is approved by MUIS
SPHM that has been revoked must be submitted to the MUIS
